Welcome to the Regenerative Plasticity Lab

Welcome to the Regenerative Plasticity Lab at Kennesaw State University. Led by Dr. Justin Varholick, we study how structural regeneration interacts with the environment, neuroplasticity, and behavior following injury. Our interdisciplinary research aims to reveal how organisms recover ‚Äî from skin to brain ‚Äî to improve regenerative medicine and rehabilitation strategies.

About the Lab

Our Mission

We investigate the plasticity of wound healing by integrating behavioral biology, developmental neuroscience, and regenerative physiology. Our long-term goal is to translate insights from highly regenerative species into therapies for humans.

Background

Founded in Fall 2025 by Dr. Justin Varholick, the Regenerative Plasticity Lab builds on a unique blend of behavioral science and regenerative biology. Our current focus explores how post-injury behavior and neuroplasticity shape healing trajectories across development and species.

Our Team

Justin Varholick, PhD

Principal Investigator

Dr. Varholick holds a Ph.D. in Biomedical Science/Applied Ethology from the University of Bern and an M.S. in Biopsychology from UNC Greensboro. He trained in regenerative biology during his postdoc under Prof. Malcolm Maden at the University of Florida. His work has contributed to understanding the phenotypic variability in tissue regeneration and establishing behavioral frameworks for studying tissue regeneration in animal models.

Currently recruiting undergraduate and graduate researchers.
